A well-designed four-bedroom semi-detached duplex can indeed minimize maintenance costs and environmental impact while incorporating architectural appeal. Some design considerations that we at Ebison consider:

  • Energy-efficient features: Solar panels, insulation, double glazing, and energy-efficient appliances.
  • Sustainable materials: Using eco-friendly materials for construction, such as reclaimed wood, low-VOC paints, and sustainable insulation.
  • Smart home technology: Integrating smart systems for energy management, security, and convenience.
  • Durable construction: High-quality materials and craftsmanship to reduce long-term maintenance.
  • Green spaces: Incorporating gardens, green roofs, or outdoor spaces for recreation and environmental benefits.
  • Natural light and ventilation: Maximizing natural light and airflow to reduce artificial lighting and heating/cooling needs.
  • Water conservation: Installing low-flow fixtures and greywater reuse systems.
  • Waste reduction: Implementing responsible construction practices and recycling systems.
